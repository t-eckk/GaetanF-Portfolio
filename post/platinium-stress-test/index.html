Portfolio Stress Testing: A Multifactor Approach

Professional Project ————

When interning at Platinium Gestion, I developed an internal tool that would be use to perform portfolio stress testing. This tool was to be simple to use, and simple to understand. It was subject to substitute stress tests done via Bloomberg’s PORT function. The tool was developed using Excel and VBA, in line with the softwares in-use within the company.

Key takeaways

  • Simple models can have similar performances to black box models
  • Using 80% confidence intervals can lead to more interpretable results when stressing portfolios
  • Appropriate factors are especially important when stress testing multi-asset portfolios

Model

The tool used a linear multifactor model to stress portfolios. Stressed portfolio returns were modelled as:

$$ \hat{R_P} = \sum_{i=1}^k w_i \hat{R_i} $$

Where:
$$ w_i \text{ is the weight of asset }i \text{, } \text{ k is the number of holdings } \text{, and } $$

$$ \hat{R_i} = \hat{\alpha_i} + \sum_{j=1}^{n}\hat{\beta}_{i,j} F_j $$
$$ F_j \text{ is factor }j \text{, } \hat{\beta}_{i,j}\text{ is the estimated coefficient relative to }F_j \text{, and } $$
$$ \hat{\alpha_i} \text{ is the estimated alpha of the portfolio.}$$

Factor exposured were determined at asset level in order to take into consideration the potential exposure drifts of multi-asset funds. The tool allowed the portfolio manager to choose up to five different factors, ranging from equity indices up to gold, with fixed income indices in between. The PM would then assign different shocks to each factor and obtained an estimation of the performance of the fund given the shock. In addition, the PM could conduct further stress test on the differents currencies owned by the fund.

Comparison with PORT

In order to compare the significance of the model compared to Bloomberg PORT, I came up with a simple 3 steps methodology:

  • Extract Bloomberg PORT estimated results
  • Compute the model for each stock and retain 99%, 95% and 80% confidence intervals
  • Assess *** if Bloomberg’s estimation falls within the 99% interval, ** if it falls within the 95% interval, and * if it falls within the 80% interval.

Below is the results of our significance assessment, asset names have been hidden:

Approximately 70% of the holdings had at least one star of significance. For the considered stress test (15 to 20% equity drawdowns topped by a 10% drawdown in oil), our model estimated -7% for the fund performance, whereas PORT estimated -6.2%.

While the difference is noticeable, it is not drastic. Furthermore, our model gives a more pessimistic outcome, which may be preferrable when trying to assess the worst (although it should remain a reasonable figure).
